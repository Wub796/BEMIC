Meet Our Founder

Dr. Haiping Hao

Dr. Haiping Hao is an accomplished educator, researcher, and curriculum developer with over 15 years of leadership in early childhood mathematics and bilingual education. She holds a Ph.D. in Curriculum & Instruction with a specialization in Early Mathematics Education and completed postdoctoral research at Texas A&M University. Having led educational initiatives in both Shanghai and the United States, she brings a unique global perspective to early STEM learning.

  • Ph.D. in Curriculum and Instruction (Early Math Education)
  • Postdoctoral Research at Texas A&M University
  • 12+ Years of Educational Leadership in Shanghai, China
  • Child Development Associate (CDA) Credential & Texas Leadership Candidate
  • Experienced in National Council of Teachers of Mathematics (NCTM) Projects

Four Core Pillars

Integrating language, logic, and professional coaching for holistic educational impact.

Early Mathematics

Developmentally appropriate early math for children ages 3-8 focusing on number sense, spatial awareness, and play-based STEM exploration.

Bilingual Immersion

Dual Chinese-English immersion designed to strengthen cognitive flexibility, language acquisition, and cultural competency.

Teacher Training

Professional workshops, curriculum coaching, and certification pathways to empower early childhood educators.

Parent Education

Home-based learning frameworks and parent workshops supporting school readiness and social-emotional growth.

The Science of Early Learning

Why Bilingual Early Mathematics Matters

01

STEM Predictability

Research consistently shows that early childhood mathematics skills are the strongest predictor of later academic achievement, including reading and literacy.

02

Cognitive Flexibility

Bilingual immersion strengthens executive function, enhances working memory, and develops superior problem-solving skills starting in early childhood.

03

Teacher Capacity

High-quality early STEM learning depends directly on teacher training. BEMIC bridges the educator gap with research-backed workshops.
